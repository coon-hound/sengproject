Background & Formative Experiences

Born in Nagasaki, Japan (1954)

Moved to England at age 5

"I've had this curious relationship with Japan. I've had this very strong Japanese background at home, but outside I was totally immersed in English culture."

Studied English and Philosophy at University of Kent

Initially aspired to be a songwriter and musician

Worked as a grouse beater for the Queen Mother at Balmoral Castle

Writing Style & Technique

"I've never been a great writer of prose. I can't do it. It's not out of choice." (Guardian, 2025)

Master of first-person narration

  • Creates unreliable narrators who engage in self-deception
  • Establishes distinctive atmospheric qualities
  • Characters struggle with memory and emotional repression
"I have to play to my strengths and not to my weaknesses."

Genre-crossing novelist: historical, science fiction, fantasy

Known for subtle, restrained emotion and unresolved tensions

Key Works & Themes

Major Themes:

  • Memory and its unreliability
  • Self-deception and emotional repression
  • Mortality and what makes us human
  • Duty versus personal desire
  • Isolation and missed opportunities
"Somewhere, irrationally, we can't quite accept our fate, and we long for this special dispensation."
